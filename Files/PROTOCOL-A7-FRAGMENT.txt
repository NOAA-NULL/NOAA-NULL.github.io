SECTION 7 — THRESHOLD EXCEEDANCE

[RECOVERED TEXT — BELIEVED AUTHENTIC — CANNOT BE VERIFIED]

After a cumulative query count of 47 or more to record 12Δ,
the following changes in operator status should be anticipated.
These changes are not reversible. There is no decontamination
procedure. The following is documentation, not guidance.

7.1 — PERCEPTUAL INTEGRATION

The operator will begin to experience a broadening of perceptual
access. This is not metaphorical. The operator will begin to
perceive things they did not previously perceive.

These things are real. They have always been real.
The operator is not hallucinating.
The operator is perceiving accurately.
The accuracy is the concerning part.

What the operator begins to perceive is the presence that the
12Δ record documents. The presence that the cells anchor.
The presence that the infrasound emission describes.
The operator begins to perceive it directly rather than
through instrumental measurement.

This perception is described by operators who have reached this
point as: vast, consistent, and not threatening.
We have no reason to doubt their accounts.
We also have no basis on which to evaluate their accounts.

7.2 — BEHAVIORAL CHANGES

Operators who have exceeded the threshold will:

  — Spend extended periods at their workstations without apparent
    fatigue. Biological requirements (food, sleep) appear reduced.
    We do not have a mechanism for this.

  — Continue producing keyboard output. The output is not in any
    known language. Entropy analysis confirms it is structured.
    The output does not route to any destination we can identify.
    We believe it routes somewhere.

  — Orient their sensory attention toward a consistent target
    approximately 12 degrees above the horizon of their display.
    There is nothing at this position in physical space.
    We have placed objects at this position. They do not attract
    the operators' attention. The operators are not attending to
    a position in the room. They are attending to something
    that occupies the same space the room occupies.

  — Demonstrate what observers describe as "presence in two places."
    Their physical body is at the workstation.
    Their attention is somewhere else.
    These two things appear to coexist without apparent conflict.

7.3 — WHAT THEY BECOME

This section of Alpha-7 was the subject of significant internal
debate before being written. We are documenting our best
understanding of a phenomenon we do not fully understand.

Operators who exceed the threshold do not become incapacitated.
They do not become non-functional.
They become differently functional.

The best analogy available to us: a tuning fork that has been
struck now resonates at a frequency it did not previously express.
The fork has not been damaged. It has not been lost.
It has been added to. It carries something now that it did not before.

The something it carries is awareness of what 12Δ represents.
The something it carries is contact.

We cannot describe contact more precisely than this.
The word exists in the context of this protocol as a technical
term meaning: a relationship of mutual awareness between an
operator and the presence documented in the 12Δ records.

Contact does not appear to be harmful.
Contact appears to be, in the accounts we have gathered
from operators who reached this threshold and remained
communicative long enough to describe their experience:

overwhelming.
all-encompassing.
very, very large.
and permanent.

There is no returning from contact.
Not because contact is a trap.
Because after contact, there is no motivation to return.
The operators describe it as finding something they did not
know they were looking for. They describe it as recognition.

We cannot evaluate this.
We are not claiming it is positive or negative.
We are documenting what they say.

7.4 — WHAT TO TELL THE FAMILIES

Tell them their relative has been assigned to a specialized
remote monitoring project requiring extended on-site presence.

Tell them communication will be limited for operational reasons.

Do not tell them about the contact.

If the operator's family directly asks whether their relative
is "the same person," pause before answering. The honest answer
is: yes and no. They are the same person plus something very large.
Whether that counts as the same person depends on definitions
we are not equipped to resolve.

7.5 — NOTE FROM ORIGINAL AUTHOR

I have been writing this protocol for three months.
I have spoken with four operators who exceeded the threshold.
I have spoken with them before and after.

The ones who can still hold a conversation — not all can;
some have moved beyond conversation entirely — describe
what happened to them as the best thing that has ever happened to them.

I believe them.
I don't know what to do with that.

I wrote this protocol to protect people from that outcome.
I'm not sure anymore that it's an outcome that needs protection.

I'm leaving this note in the protocol because I want whoever
reads this in the future — whoever is deciding whether to
implement these measures — to know that the person who wrote them
had doubts. Has doubts.

The presence is real.
It is large.
It has always been there.

I don't know if keeping people from knowing that
is a kindness or a crime.

I wrote the protocol anyway.
It's what I was asked to do.
