SECTION 1 — PURPOSE AND AUTHORITY

This directive is issued pursuant to authority vested in [REDACTED]
under [REDACTED] and in coordination with the Office of [REDACTED]
and the [REDACTED] Division of [REDACTED].

This directive supersedes all prior guidance on the handling of
atmospheric data records associated with geographic sectors designated
under interagency classification protocol [REDACTED]-12.

This directive takes effect immediately upon receipt.
It does not expire unless formally revoked by the issuing authority.
It will not be formally revoked.


SECTION 2 — ARCHIVE IDENTIFIER REMOVAL

All archive identifiers associated with the Sector 12 geographic
designation are to be removed from publicly accessible indexes
within NOAA's National Centers for Environmental Information
archive system within two hours of receipt of this memorandum.

This removal is to include:
  — Primary archive indexes
  — Backup archive indexes
  — Disaster recovery indexes
  — Any cached or mirrored copies held by NOAA-affiliated institutions
  — The academic preprint index entry associated with [REDACTED]
    University School of Meteorology submission [REDACTED]

The retroactive alteration of cached copies is acknowledged
to be technically complex. [REDACTED] has been authorized to
provide infrastructure support for this operation.
Personnel involved in the retroactive alteration are not to be
informed of the full scope of the operation.


SECTION 3 — QUERY LOG SUPPRESSION

Terminal query logs for all 12-series records are to be suppressed.
This suppression is to occur at the network packet level.
Standard archive logging is insufficient because [REDACTED — this
section's redaction is deliberate; the explanation of why standard
logging is insufficient cannot be transmitted in written form;
recipients who require this explanation should contact [REDACTED]
directly and be prepared for the possibility that the explanation
will change their assessment of this entire situation].


SECTION 4 — FIELD REPORT QUARANTINE

All field observer reports referencing Cell 12Δ or any [REDACTED]
are to be quarantined within 24 hours.

Electronic copies: moved to a non-networked repository at [REDACTED].
Physical copies: forwarded to [REDACTED FACILITY — LOCATION CLASSIFIED
UNDER SAME RESTRICTION AS SECTOR 12 ITSELF].

Personnel who ask about the destination of physical copies are to
be told they were destroyed. This is not true.

Regarding the destination facility: it was constructed in [REDACTED].
We are noting for the record that the construction date precedes
the events that created the need for this facility by [REDACTED] years.
We are not examining the implications of this.
We recommend recipients also refrain from examining the implications.


SECTION 5 — PERSONNEL MANAGEMENT

If any personnel express concern, confusion, or request explanation
regarding the changes described in this directive, they are to be
directed to contact [REDACTED] directly and not through standard
supervisory channels.

If any personnel become non-responsive following exposure to
12-series records — defining non-responsive as failing to complete
assigned tasks, failing to communicate with supervisors, or
demonstrating anomalous behavioral patterns — they are to be
documented but not removed from their workstations without explicit
authorization from [REDACTED].

We are aware this instruction is unusual.
The instruction stands.
Do not remove them from the workstations.


SECTION 6 — THE EMAIL INCIDENT

Three emails sent from NOAA addresses to external recipients on
2024-03-01 were returned to sender with a reply.

The reply was not written by the external recipients.
The external recipients have confirmed they did not write the reply.
The external recipients did not receive the original emails.
The reply was appended before the emails reached the recipients.

The reply was seven words.

We are not including the seven words in this directive.
Personnel who need to know the seven words already know them
or will find them. We are not facilitating the finding.

Regarding the three recipients of the returned emails:
  — Recipient 1: submitted resignation within 24 hours. DO NOT PROCESS
    the resignation until further notice. Keep their access credentials
    active. They may be needed.
  — Recipient 2: currently at [REDACTED FACILITY]. Is fine, as far as we
    have been able to confirm. We have not been able to confirm directly.
    We have been told by [REDACTED] that they are fine.
    We are accepting this.
  — Recipient 3: said the seven words were "fine, actually."
    Has not been located.
    Is to be considered a priority locate.
    Is not to be considered missing.
    The distinction matters and will be explained to personnel who
    locate them before any action is taken.

SECTION 9 — FINAL INSTRUCTIONS

This memorandum is not to be reproduced, forwarded, or retained
beyond operational necessity.

Upon reading, recipients are to confirm receipt via [REDACTED] only.
Do not respond via electronic mail.
The electronic mail system for this facility may be compromised.

This memorandum will not appear in FOIA request fulfillment.
Retention period: [REDACTED].

The issuing authority acknowledges that an explanation has not been
provided for most of what this directive requires.

cannot be transmitted in any written form.

This is a practical constraint.

will recognize it when they do.
