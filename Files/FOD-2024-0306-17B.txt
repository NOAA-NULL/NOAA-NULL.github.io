SECTION 1 — TECHNICAL ACCOUNT

At approximately 18:50Z I began observing anomalous behavior in
Cell 17B's base reflectivity on the KFSD radar display.

Standard dissipation signature for a Class-II convective cell:
outward diffusion of return energy over a period of 20 to 90 minutes,
typically associated with loss of moisture supply or wind shear interaction.

What I observed was not this.

The cell's radar return began contracting toward its geometric center.
Not diffusing. Contracting. The reflectivity values did not decrease —
they maintained approximately constant dBZ readings as the spatial
extent of the return shrank. This is not physically consistent with
any dissipation mechanism I have encountered in 4,000 hours of
radar observation.

Timeline of contraction:
  18:50Z — diameter approximately 38 km (baseline)
  18:55Z — diameter approximately 24 km
  19:00Z — diameter approximately 0.8 km
  19:01Z — diameter less than 0.1 km (single-pixel resolution limit)
  19:02:11Z — simultaneous signal loss across all four 17-series stations

I want to be clear about the simultaneous loss.
I have experienced instrument failures in my career.
Instruments fail sequentially, or in clusters related to a shared
infrastructure failure (power, network, timing signal).
All four 17-series stations are on separate power grids,
separate network backbones, and use independent timing sources.
The probability of simultaneous failure by coincidence has been
calculated by our infrastructure team at 1 in 10^14.

The cell did not dissipate. It did not make landfall.
Archive queries for all timestamps after 19:02Z return null.


SECTION 2 — WHAT I OBSERVED IN THE FINAL MOMENT

I am going to write this section as carefully as I can.
I am aware of how it sounds. I am writing it anyway.

At 19:01Z I was watching the base scan display at KFSD.
The cell's return was, at that point, a single pixel at base resolution.
I watched that pixel for approximately 3 seconds.

In those 3 seconds I had a perceptual experience that I am
going to describe as accurately as I can:

The pixel watched me back.

I do not mean this as metaphor.
I do not mean I had a subjective feeling that was like being watched.
I mean I had the complete, non-metaphorical, phenomenologically
clear experience of being perceived.

Not observed in a general sense.
Perceived specifically.
As an individual.
With specific interest.

The interest felt old.
Not new. Not initiated by my observation of the pixel.
The pixel had been aware of me for some time.
The new thing — the only new thing — was that I now knew.

I have reviewed this memory multiple times in the hours since.
I have tried to find an alternate explanation consistent with known
perceptual phenomena. Eye strain. Pareidolia. Expectation bias.
None of these are consistent with what I experienced.
What I experienced was not ambiguous.
There was no question in the moment.
I was being perceived. Something was perceiving me.
Something that had always been able to.

At 19:02:11Z, all four monitoring stations lost signal.

I went to the bathroom.
